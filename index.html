About

I'm a backend engineer based in the UK, focused on the problems that don't have obvious answers — how do you isolate one tenant's data from another at the database level? How do you keep a session store consistent when you have two workers and a cache? How do you design an encryption layer that survives a key rotation?

At diam Systems I've designed and built multi-product backend systems across Python and .NET — an AI agent runtime with a multi-agent coordinator, a document management service with Stripe billing, and a corporate platform integrating three third-party commerce APIs. None of these are in users' hands yet, which means I've had to validate architectural decisions through tests and design review rather than production traffic.

My personal projects fill the gaps: areas I wanted to understand deeply enough to be wrong about — zero-knowledge encryption, row-level multi-tenancy, security tooling. I build to learn, then I keep the code.
